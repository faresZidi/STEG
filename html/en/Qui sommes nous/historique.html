STEG: Yesterday, Today & Tomorrow

On the eve of independence, the electricity sector was managed by seven concessionary companies responsible for supplying the main regions of the country, with a total installed capacity of nearly 100 MW and a production of around 240 GWh.

In 1962, to harmonize the electricity and gas sectors, the Tunisian State established the Tunisian Electricity and Gas Company (STEG), entrusting it with the production, transportation, and distribution of electricity and gas.

After 60 years, the electricity sector has achieved impressive milestones:

  • The overall electrification rate increased from 21% to 99.8%.
  • Rural electrification reached 99.5%.
  • Installed capacity rose to 6,014 MW.
  • National production reached 19,520 GWh.
  • The number of electricity customers is 4,419,053.

As for gas, consumption reached 5,436 Ktoe, and the number of customers increased from 25,000 to 1,021,051 (compared to 989,600 in 2021).
